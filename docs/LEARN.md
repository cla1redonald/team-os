# Learning path — become the master in the room
*Building the thing and knowing the thing are different. ~2–3 hours to go from "I built a thing" to "I can defend every claim in this room."*

## Read in this order
1. **[EXPERT-BRIEFING.md](EXPERT-BRIEFING.md)** — the whole argument in one pass. Read it twice.
2. **[research/01_formats-and-layers.md](research/01_formats-and-layers.md)** — internalise the **four layers**. If you know nothing else cold, know this.
3. **[research/04_beads.md](research/04_beads.md)** — the Beads/Dolt story; it's your "I'm current" flex.
4. **[research/02_non-eng-practitioners.md](research/02_non-eng-practitioners.md)** — your thesis evidence; memorise 3 names per function.
5. **[research/03_vendors-build-vs-buy.md](research/03_vendors-build-vs-buy.md)** — for the debate. Know the permission-aware-retrieval gap.
6. Skim `bundle/` — actually read `index.md` and two runbooks so you can narrate them.

## The 8 things you must be able to say without notes
1. The four layers and which one is the gap outside engineering.
2. What OKF is (markdown, one concept/file, only `type` required, cross-linked graph) and that it's 3 weeks old.
3. Why "isn't this just a wiki?" is the right question and the convergence answer.
4. What Beads is, the "50 First Dates" problem, and that it's **Dolt-backed now**.
5. The builders-vs-vendors finding (eng/PM/marketing have people; sales/HR/finance have vendors).
6. The #1 reason to buy: permission-aware retrieval.
7. Memory ≠ knowledge (and where Beads sits).
8. Your own proof: the agents, skills, and knowledge systems you actually run.

## Practice reps (do these out loud)
- **The 90-second thesis** — deliver it cold, no notes.
- **The demo narration** — run [DEMO.md](DEMO.md) end-to-end talking through *why* each step works, twice.
- **Hostile Q&A** — get someone (or an agent) to fire the §9 questions at you; answer each in <30s.
- **The whiteboard** — draw the four layers from memory in under a minute.

## If you only have 30 minutes before you go on
Read EXPERT-BRIEFING.md §1, §2, §5, §8, §9. That's the thesis, the model, the proof, the script, and the defence. Everything else is depth you can gesture at.

## Honesty rules (protect your credibility)
- OKF is promising, not proven — never oversell a 3-week-old spec.
- Cite Beads as Dolt-backed.
- Northwind numbers are synthetic; say so if asked.
- Don't quote a star count or a LinkedIn URL you haven't checked that morning — cite the person.
